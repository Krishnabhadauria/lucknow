History of Lucknow

Lucknow, the capital city of the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh, has a rich and vibrant history that spans over several centuries. From being a small town to becoming a center of culture, politics, and art, Lucknow has witnessed the rise and fall of various dynasties and played a significant role in shaping the history of India. Lucknow's history can be traced back to ancient times. The area around present-day Lucknow was ruled by various empires and dynasties, including the Mauryas, Kushans, Guptas, and the Sultanate of Delhi. The city came under the control of the Delhi Sultanate in the 13th century and later passed into the hands of the Mughal Empire in the 16th century.During the Mughal era, Lucknow gained prominence as a strategic center. It served as a provincial capital and a military outpost. The golden era of Lucknow's history began with the rise of the Nawabs of Awadh. The city experienced tremendous growth and development under their rule. The Nawabs were known for their refined taste, patronage of arts, literature, and architecture, and the creation of a unique cultural blend known as "Lucknowi Tehzeeb." In 1856, the British East India Company annexed Awadh and dethroned the last Nawab, Wajid Ali Shah. The annexation led to widespread resentment among the Indian soldiers and civilians, eventually culminating in the Indian Rebellion of 1857, also known as the First War of Independence. Today, Lucknow stands as a blend of its rich historical past and modern developments. It is known for its beautiful gardens, historical monuments, educational institutions, and the warm hospitality of its people. The city continues to preserve and celebrate its cultural heritage while embracing progress and urbanization.

Culture of Lucknow

The culture of Lucknow reflects its rich historical heritage, especially the Nawabi era, combined with the modern influences of a cosmopolitan city. Here's a brief overview of the current culture of Lucknow:

  • Tehzeeb (Etiquette and Manners): Lucknow is known for its "Lucknowi Tehzeeb," which refers to the city's traditional etiquette and mannerisms. People in Lucknow are known for their courteousness, politeness, and warm hospitality. Respect for elders and a sense of cultural refinement are deeply ingrained in the local culture.

  • Language and Literature: Urdu and Hindi are the primary languages spoken in Lucknow. The city has a long-standing association with Urdu literature and poetry. Many renowned poets and writers have hailed from Lucknow, and mushairas (poetry recitals) are popular cultural events.

  • Cuisine: Lucknow is famous for its mouth-watering Awadhi cuisine. The city is known for its delectable kebabs, biryanis, and traditional sweets like the iconic "Lucknowi paan." Tunday Kebabi, Idris ki Biryani, and Prakash Kulfi are some of the iconic food establishments in Lucknow.

  • Music and Dance: Classical music and dance forms hold a significant place in Lucknow's cultural landscape. The city is renowned for its contribution to the classical dance form of Kathak. Numerous music and dance academies and cultural institutions promote and preserve these art forms.

  • Architecture and Heritage: Lucknow's architectural heritage is evident in its grand historical structures like the Bara Imambara, Chhota Imambara, and Rumi Darwaza. The architectural style of Lucknow reflects a fusion of Indo-Islamic and European influences. The city's heritage buildings and monuments continue to attract tourists and serve as a reminder of its glorious past.

  • Handicrafts and Embroidery: Lucknow is renowned for its exquisite chikankari embroidery, a traditional art form that involves intricate white threadwork on fabric. Chikankari garments and textiles are highly valued and sought after. The city is also known for its zardozi embroidery, pottery, and handicrafts.

Art in Lucknow

Chikankari

Chikankari is a delicate and intricate embroidery style that originated in Lucknow. It involves white threadwork on fine fabrics like muslin and silk, creating beautiful patterns and designs. Chikankari garments, including sarees, kurtas, and dupattas, are highly prized for their craftsmanship.

Zardozi embroidery
